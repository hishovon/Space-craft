Welcome to Space-Analysis Area

Space is a vast and mysterious expanse that surrounds our planet Earth and extends to unimaginable distances. It is a vacuum devoid of air, sound, and gravity, filled with celestial objects like stars, planets, galaxies,
and more. The study of space, known as astronomy, has allowed us to gain insights into the origins and workings of the universe. Space exploration has seen significant milestones, including the first human landing
on the moon. Satellites orbiting Earth provide crucial communication, navigation, and weather data. The extreme conditions of space, such as extreme temperatures and the absence of air, pose challenges for
human exploration. Nonetheless, our fascination with space continues to drive scientific research and inspire new discoveries about our cosmic surroundings.

Earth

Earth—our home planet—is the only place we know of so far that's inhabited by living things. It's also the only planet in our solar system with liquid water on the surface.

Jupiter

Jupiter is more than twice as massive than the other planets of our solar system combined. The giant planet's Great Red Spot is a centuries-old storm bigger than Earth.

Lune

The "traditional" moon count most people are familiar with stands at 290: One moon for Earth; two for Mars; 95 at Jupiter; 146 at Saturn; 27 at Uranus; 14 at Neptune.

Saturn

Adorned with a dazzling, complex system of icy rings, Saturn is unique in our solar system. The other giant planets have rings, but none are as spectacular as Saturn's.

Venus

Similar in structure and size to Earth, Venus's thick atmosphere traps heat in a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet in our solar system.

Jupiter

Uranus

Uranus—seventh planet from the Sun—rotates at a nearly 90-degree angle from the plane of its orbit. This unique tilt makes Uranus appear to spin on its side.

Solar System

Our planetary system is called “the solar system” because we use the word “solar” to describe things related to our star, after the Latin word for Sun, "solis."
